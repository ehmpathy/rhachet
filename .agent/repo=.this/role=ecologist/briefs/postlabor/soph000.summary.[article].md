## .context

### the postlabor economy

automation and robots will handle most labor. this creates abundance â€” but abundance alone does not guarantee prosperity for all.

history shows that critical junctures tip societies toward either:
- **exclusive institutions** â€” where extractors capture abundance for themselves
- **inclusive institutions** â€” where abundance is distributed broadly

the iron law of oligarchy warns that social structures default toward extraction. without deliberate intervention, postlabor abundance could concentrate rather than spread.

### extractors vs distributors

nations fail when exclusive institutions capture resources; they prosper when inclusive institutions distribute them. this is the central thesis of "why nations fail" (acemoglu & robinson).

- **extractors** = those who build exclusive institutions to capture abundance
- **distributors** = those who build inclusive institutions to share abundance

the same dynamic applies to postlabor. the question is not whether abundance will exist, but whether it will be extracted or distributed.

### critical junctures

critical junctures are historical moments when societies can shift toward extraction or distribution. postlabor is such a moment.

key insight: distribution is inevitable, but the duration is variable. deliberate action accelerates the timeline.

### iron law of oligarchy

all organizations trend toward oligarchy (michels). extraction is the default. distribution requires deliberate mechanisms to counter it.

this is why rhachet exists â€” to accelerate distribution via mechanisms that only click forward.

### the keystone: brains

the brains ratchet is the most important â€” it unlocks the others.

- software spreads instantly; to copy is free
- brains can always build brawns (not the other way around)

### entropic ratchets

ratchets are entropic: they only move one direction.

- each click is irreversible
- progress compounds over time
- reversal is structurally impossible
- given enough time, ratchets will click; the only variable is duration

this is not metaphor. this is mechanism. ratchets work because they are entropic â€” like entropy, they only increase, never decrease.
